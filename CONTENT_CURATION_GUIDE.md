# Content Curation Guide for Vidhi CLAT Prep

## Overview

This guide explains how to curate current affairs content for Vidhi using The Hindu and other sources. This approach is **100% legal** and avoids copyright issues.

## Why This Approach?

1. **Legal Compliance**: We don't copy full articles; we write original CLAT-focused summaries
2. **Value Addition**: Transform news into exam-relevant content with legal analysis
3. **Quiz Integration**: Every article includes 5 MCQs for practice
4. **Source Attribution**: Always link to original articles

## Content Curation Workflow

### Step 1: Discover Articles (Admin Dashboard)

**URL**: `/admin`

1. Browse RSS feeds from The Hindu and PIB
2. Look for CLAT-relevant topics:
   - Supreme Court judgments
   - Constitutional amendments
   - Government policies
   - International relations
   - Economic updates
   - Social issues
   - Science & technology developments

3. Click "Curate" on interesting articles

### Step 2: Write CLAT-Focused Summary

**What to Include:**

```markdown
## Article Structure

### Title (Your own - don't copy)
- Make it CLAT-focused
- Clear and concise

### Excerpt (2-3 sentences)
- Summarize the key point
- Why it matters for CLAT

### Content (500-1500 words)

#### Background
- Context of the issue
- Historical perspective

#### Key Points (Bulleted)
- Main facts
- Legal aspects
- Constitutional provisions involved

#### Significance
- Why this matters for CLAT
- Related concepts students should know

#### Legal Analysis
- Constitutional articles involved
- Relevant case laws
- Legal principles

#### CLAT Connection
- Which sections this relates to (Legal Reasoning, Current Affairs, etc.)
- Types of questions that could be asked
```

### Step 3: Source Attribution

**Always include:**
- Link to original article
- Source name (The Hindu, PIB, etc.)
- Publication date

**Example:**
```
Source: The Hindu | Published: December 15, 2024
Read original: [link]
```

### Step 4: Create Quiz Questions

**5 MCQs per article**

**Question Template:**
```json
{
  "question": "Clear, specific question about the article",
  "options": [
    "Correct answer",
    "Plausible distractor",
    "Another distractor",
    "Third distractor"
  ],
  "correctAnswer": 0,
  "explanation": "Why option 1 is correct and why others are wrong",
  "marks": 1,
  "difficulty": "medium"
}
```

**Question Types:**
1. **Factual**: Direct facts from the article
2. **Analytical**: Requires understanding of implications
3. **Legal**: Related constitutional/legal provisions
4. **Comparative**: Compare with similar cases/concepts
5. **Application**: How the concept applies to scenarios

### Step 5: Categorization

**Choose appropriate:**
- **Category**: Legal & Constitutional, Economy & Business, etc.
- **Subcategory**: Supreme Court, Monetary Policy, etc.
- **Tags**: 5-7 relevant keywords
- **Importance**: High/Medium/Low
- **Read Time**: Calculate based on word count (200 words/min)

### Step 6: Review & Publish

1. **Self-Review Checklist:**
   - [ ] Title is clear and CLAT-focused
   - [ ] Excerpt summarizes key point
   - [ ] Content is 500-1500 words
   - [ ] Has bulleted key points
   - [ ] Includes legal analysis
   - [ ] Source is attributed
   - [ ] 5 quiz questions created
   - [ ] All questions have explanations
   - [ ] Grammar and spelling checked
   - [ ] No copyright violation

2. **Submit for Review**: Senior team member reviews
3. **Publish**: Goes live on platform

## Copyright & Fair Use Guidelines

### ✅ What You CAN Do:

1. **Read The Hindu article for facts**
2. **Write your own summary** in your own words
3. **Add legal analysis** (this is original content)
4. **Create quiz questions** (original assessment)
5. **Link to original article**
6. **Cite source properly**

### ❌ What You CANNOT Do:

1. **Copy full articles** word-for-word
2. **Use large blocks of text** without quotes
3. **Republish images** without permission
4. **Remove attribution**
5. **Use as primary content source**

### Fair Use Justification:

Our content qualifies as fair use because:
- **Transformative**: We add educational analysis
- **Purpose**: Educational, not commercial copying
- **Nature**: Factual news (less protected than creative works)
- **Amount**: We summarize, not copy in full
- **Effect**: We drive traffic to original source (benefit them)

## Example: Good vs. Bad Curation

### ❌ Bad Example (Copyright Violation):

```markdown
Title: Supreme Court on Electoral Bonds

[Copies entire article from The Hindu word-for-word]

Source: The Hindu
```

**Problems:**
- Direct copy-paste
- No original analysis
- No CLAT focus
- Copyright violation

### ✅ Good Example (Legal & Valuable):

```markdown
Title: Electoral Bonds Scheme Unconstitutional: SC Strikes Down Anonymous Political Funding

**Excerpt**: In a landmark judgment, the Supreme Court declared the Electoral Bonds Scheme unconstitutional, holding that anonymous political funding violates citizens' right to information under Article 19(1)(a). This ruling reinforces transparency in electoral processes.

**Content**:

Background:
The Electoral Bonds Scheme, introduced in 2018, allowed anonymous donations to political parties through special bonds sold by SBI. The scheme was challenged on grounds of...

[Original summary in your own words]

Key Holdings:
• Violates Article 19(1)(a) - Right to Information
• Anonymity affects free and fair elections
• Unlimited corporate donations problematic
[More points]

Legal Analysis:
This judgment builds on the Puttaswamy judgment (2017) which recognized...

CLAT Connection:
• Constitutional Law: Article 19(1)(a)
• Current Affairs: Electoral reforms
• Legal Reasoning: Balancing transparency vs. privacy
[More connections]

Source: The Hindu | December 15, 2024
Read original article: [link]
```

**Why This is Good:**
- Original writing
- Adds legal analysis
- CLAT-focused
- Properly attributed
- Transformative content

## FAQ

**Q: Can we use quotes from the article?**
A: Yes, short quotes (1-2 sentences) with quotation marks are fine.

**Q: How much content can we reference?**
A: Follow the 10% rule - no more than 10% of your content should be directly quoted.

**Q: What if the article is really good - can't we just use it?**
A: No. Always write original summaries. The value we provide is CLAT-focused analysis, not reproducing news.

**Q: Can we use PIB press releases fully?**
A: PIB content is government-produced and generally usable, but still write your own summary with analysis.

**Q: What about images?**
A: Don't copy images from The Hindu. Use placeholder images or create simple graphics.
